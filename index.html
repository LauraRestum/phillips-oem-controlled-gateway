WHY WE'RE HERE

Current opportunities in the federal channel

A high-velocity federal opportunity runs through Philips consumables on DIBBS today. The way it currently operates, through a fragmented set of independent resellers, leaves room for a more coordinated approach.

What this means in practice. Individual solicitations close in days. Resellers currently compete without coordination, with inconsistent pricing and varied compliance. Demand intelligence never flows back to the manufacturer. The cost of that fragmentation lands on Philips.
The structural question. Is there a version of this channel where Philips sees one consistent representative on DIBBS, one compliance standard, and one feedback loop on what federal buyers actually want? That is the conversation this proposal is designed to open.

  • Active federal contractor with SAM.gov registration and procurement experience across regulated channels.

HOW THE MODEL WORKS

What the partnership requires from both sides

For the model to work, three things need to be true on each side.
ENVISION COMMITS TO
Channel execution
  • 1
    Daily DIBBS monitoringContinuous review of solicitations against agreed Philips product codes.
  • 2
    Target 24 to 72 hour responseCompliance-verified quotes submitted inside DIBBS windows.
  • 3
    Quarterly reportingDemand intelligence and channel performance back to Philips.
PHILIPS CONTRIBUTES
Channel structure
  • 1
    A standing pricing indexMonthly or quarterly schedule on high-volume consumables, replacing ad-hoc requests.
  • 2
    A defined channel positionTiered pricing, SKU exclusivity, or authorized reseller status for the pilot period.
  • 3
    A dedicated point of contactOne person inside Philips who can respond on pricing or spec clarifications inside active bid windows.
